# Explainable Hiring Compliance: Executive Summary

## Regulatory Pressure

The banking industry faces converging compliance requirements that traditional hiring systems cannot address. Federal Reserve SR 11-7 guidance mandates explainable models with ongoing monitoring for all decision-support systems. NYC Local Law 144 requires bias audits with specific statistical thresholds, while FCRA demands specific reason codes for adverse hiring decisions.

## Our Methodology

We apply Bayesian rule reliability estimation to content-neutral resume features, creating per-skill aptitude scores with quantified uncertainty. Association rule mining discovers predictive patterns while fairness filtering removes protected attribute proxies. Each hiring decision decomposes into traceable rule contributions that satisfy both explainability and performance requirements.

## PoC Deliverables

• **Automated model cards** compliant with SR 11-7 documentation standards
• **Real-time fairness monitoring** dashboard with 4/5 rule violation alerts  
• **FCRA-compliant adverse action notices** generated from rule contributions
• **Quarterly bias audit reports** ready for NYC LL144 submission
• **Per-skill aptitude explanations** for candidate feedback and manager guidance
• **Challenger model framework** for ongoing model validation
• **Production deployment** in customer VPC with full audit logging

## What We Explicitly Do NOT Promise

• **Production-ready performance guarantees** — PoC validates methodology on customer data
• **Elimination of hiring bias** — we provide measurement and mitigation tools
• **Specific AUC targets above 0.62** — performance bracketed during PoC validation
• **Legal compliance certification** — independent legal review required for all outputs
• **Multi-tenant SaaS deployment** — candidate data remains in customer environment
